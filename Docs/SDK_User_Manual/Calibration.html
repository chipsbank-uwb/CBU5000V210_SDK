UWB Calibration

Calibration Bias Setting

Accurate calibration is essential for ensuring reliable angle and distance measurements in UWB systems.

During calibration:

  • Devices are tested under controlled conditions (e.g., fixed distance, level placement).

  • At least 100 measurements are collected per test condition.

  • The median value of these samples is used as the bias input to reduce the effect of noise and outliers.

Phase Difference of Arrival Bias

PDOA bias corrects angle estimation errors based on phase differences between antenna pairs, measured at the 0-degree reference.

\[\begin{split}\text{PD01 Bias Input} = \text{Median PD01 at } 0^\circ \\ \text{PD02 Bias Input} = \text{Median PD02 at } 0^\circ \\ \text{PD12 Bias Input} = \text{Median PD12 at } 0^\circ\end{split}\]

Setup:

  • Devices are placed 100 cm apart.

  • Both are aligned at the same height and oriented at 0° azimuth and elevation.

  • At least 100 samples are taken for each PD value, and the median is used.

Where:

  • Each input is the median phase difference for the specified antenna pair.

  • These values are stored and applied by the SDK to correct azimuth and elevation angles.

Ranging Bias

Ranging bias accounts for the error between the actual distance and the distance reported by the UWB system.

\[\text{Ranging Bias} = \text{Actual Distance} - \text{Median Detected Distance}\]

Setup:

  • Devices are positioned at a known reference distance (e.g., 100 cm).

  • At least 100 measurements are taken to compute the median detected distance.

Where:

  • The unit of bias is in centimeters (cm).

  • The resulting bias is applied within the SDK for distance correction.
